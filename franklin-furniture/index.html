Uncompromising durability

At Franklin Furniture, we understand that durability is a key factor in choosing furniture. Our pieces are built to last, using the finest materials and construction techniques to ensure that they can withstand the rigors of everyday life.

  • Solid wood frames:

    The frames of our furniture are made from solid wood, which is known for its strength and durability. Solid wood is also less likely to warp or crack over time, ensuring that your furniture will maintain its structural integrity for years to come.

  • Eight-way hand-tied springs:

    Our upholstered furniture features eight-way hand-tied springs, which provide superior support and comfort. This traditional method of spring construction ensures that the springs are evenly distributed and will not sag or lose their shape over time.

  • Corner-blocked construction:

    Our furniture is constructed using corner-blocking, which is a technique that adds strength and stability to the frame. Corner-blocking prevents the frame from twisting or racking, ensuring that your furniture will remain sturdy and durable for years to come.

  • Kiln-dried wood:

    We use kiln-dried wood in our furniture to ensure that it is properly seasoned and free of moisture. Kiln-drying prevents the wood from warping, cracking, or shrinking over time, ensuring that your furniture will maintain its shape and beauty for years to come.

Franklin Furniture's commitment to uncompromising durability means that our furniture is built to last. Our pieces are designed to withstand the rigors of everyday life and maintain their beauty for generations to come.

FAQ

Welcome to the Garden Furniture FAQ section!

Question 1: What materials are commonly used for garden furniture?
Answer: Garden furniture is typically made from a variety of materials, including wood, metal, plastic, and wicker. Each material has its own unique advantages and disadvantages. Wood is a classic choice for garden furniture, offering a natural and timeless look. Metal furniture is durable and easy to maintain, but it can be prone to rust if not properly cared for. Plastic furniture is lightweight and weather-resistant, but it may not be as durable as other materials. Wicker furniture adds a touch of elegance to your garden, but it requires regular maintenance to keep it looking its best.

Question 2: How do I choose the right garden furniture for my needs?
Answer: When choosing garden furniture, there are a few things to consider. First, think about the size of your garden and the amount of space you have available. You also need to decide what type of furniture you need. Do you need a dining set, a lounge set, or both? Once you know what you need, you can start shopping for furniture that is the right size and style for your garden.

Tips

Follow these tips to keep your garden furniture looking its best:

Tip 1: Clean your garden furniture regularly.

Regular cleaning will help to remove dirt, pollen, and other debris that can build up on your furniture over time. Use a mild soap and water solution and a soft cloth to clean your furniture. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, as these can damage the finish.

Tip 2: Protect your garden furniture from the elements.

If you live in an area with harsh weather conditions, it's important to protect your garden furniture from the elements. Cover your furniture with a tarp or furniture covers when it's not in use. You can also apply a protective sealant to your furniture to help protect it from the sun and rain.

Tip 3: Store your garden furniture properly during the off-season.

If you live in an area with cold winters, it's important to store your garden furniture properly during the off-season. Clean your furniture thoroughly and then store it in a dry, sheltered place. You can also wrap your furniture in blankets or towels to protect it from dust and moisture.

Tip 4: Inspect your garden furniture regularly for damage.

Regularly inspect your garden furniture for any signs of damage. This includes checking for cracks, loose screws, or rust. If you find any damage, repair it promptly to prevent further damage.

Closing Paragraph for Tips:

By following these tips, you can help to keep your garden furniture looking its best for years to come.
