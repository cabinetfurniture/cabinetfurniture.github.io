Choose weather-resistant materials

Since summerhouses are often exposed to the elements, it's important to choose furniture made from weather-resistant materials. This will ensure that your furniture lasts for many seasons to come and can withstand the rigors of outdoor use.

There are a variety of weather-resistant materials available for summerhouse furniture, including:

  • Teak: Teak is a naturally weather-resistant wood that is known for its durability and longevity. It is also resistant to rot and insects, making it a great choice for outdoor furniture.
  • Cedar: Cedar is another weather-resistant wood that is known for its beautiful reddish-brown color. It is also resistant to rot and insects, and it has a pleasant, natural scent.
  • Polyethylene: Polyethylene is a synthetic material that is commonly used for outdoor furniture. It is lightweight, durable, and resistant to fading and cracking. Polyethylene furniture is also easy to clean and maintain.
  • Wicker: Wicker is a natural material that is made from the stems of rattan or willow plants. Wicker furniture is lightweight and durable, and it can withstand most weather conditions. However, it is important to protect wicker furniture from prolonged exposure to direct sunlight and rain.
  • Aluminum: Aluminum is a lightweight and durable metal that is commonly used for outdoor furniture. It is rust-resistant and easy to clean, making it a good choice for summerhouses near the beach or in areas with high humidity.

When choosing weather-resistant materials for your summerhouse furniture, consider the climate in your area and the amount of exposure to the elements that your furniture will receive. You should also think about the style of furniture that you want and the overall aesthetic of your summerhouse.

By choosing weather-resistant materials, you can ensure that your summerhouse furniture will last for many years and provide you with years of enjoyment.

Consider multi-functional pieces

Multi-functional furniture is a great way to save space and add flexibility to your summerhouse. Look for pieces that can serve multiple purposes, such as ottomans with built-in storage or coffee tables with lift-top surfaces that can be used as dining tables. You could also consider modular furniture that can be reconfigured to create different seating arrangements.

Here are some examples of multi-functional summerhouse furniture pieces:

  • Daybeds: Daybeds can be used for lounging, sleeping, or even as extra seating. They are a great option for small summerhouses or for creating a cozy reading nook.
  • Storage benches: Storage benches provide a place to sit and store items such as blankets, pillows, or gardening supplies. They can also be used as coffee tables or footrests.
  • Nesting tables: Nesting tables are a great way to save space and add flexibility to your summerhouse. They can be used as end tables, coffee tables, or even extra dining space.
  • Folding chairs: Folding chairs are a great option for summerhouses that are used for entertaining. They can be easily stored away when not in use and brought out when you have guests.
  • Hammocks: Hammocks are a classic choice for summerhouses. They provide a comfortable place to relax and enjoy the outdoors.

By incorporating multi-functional pieces into your summerhouse, you can make the most of the space you have and create a versatile and inviting space for relaxation and enjoyment.

With a little planning, you can create a summerhouse that is both stylish and functional, providing you with a relaxing and enjoyable retreat all season long.

Don't overcrowd the space

One of the most important things to keep in mind when furnishing your summerhouse is to avoid overcrowding the space. A cluttered and cramped summerhouse will be uncomfortable and uninviting. Instead, aim for a more minimalist approach, with only the essential pieces of furniture that you need.

  • Choose furniture that is appropriately sized for your space. Oversized furniture will make your summerhouse feel smaller and more cramped.
  • Leave enough space between pieces of furniture so that people can move around comfortably. You should also leave enough space for walkways and doorways.
  • Avoid using too many bulky or heavy pieces of furniture. This will make your summerhouse feel more cluttered and less airy.
  • If you have a small summerhouse, consider using folding or stackable furniture. This type of furniture can be easily stored away when not in use, freeing up space.

By following these tips, you can avoid overcrowding your summerhouse and create a space that is both comfortable and inviting.

Layer rugs for warmth and texture

Rugs are a great way to add warmth and texture to your summerhouse. They can also help to define different areas of the space and create a more inviting atmosphere.

Here are some tips for layering rugs in your summerhouse:

  • Start with a large, neutral rug as the foundation. This will help to ground the space and create a cohesive look.
  • Add a smaller, patterned rug on top of the neutral rug. This will add a pop of color and personality to the space.
  • Layer a third rug, such as a sheepskin or cowhide rug, on top of the other two rugs. This will add warmth and texture to the space.
  • Experiment with different rug sizes, shapes, and textures to create a unique and inviting look.

Rugs can also be used to define different areas of your summerhouse. For example, you could use a large rug to create a cozy seating area, and a smaller rug to define the dining area.

By layering rugs in your summerhouse, you can create a space that is both stylish and inviting. Rugs can also help to keep your summerhouse warm and cozy, even on chilly evenings.

Incorporate outdoor lighting

Proper lighting is essential for creating a comfortable and inviting atmosphere in your summerhouse, especially during the evening hours. Here are some tips for incorporating outdoor lighting into your summerhouse:

  • Use a combination of natural and artificial light. Natural light is always the best option, so make sure your summerhouse has plenty of windows and doors to let in the sunshine. Artificial light can be used to supplement natural light in the evening hours or on cloudy days.
  • Choose outdoor lighting fixtures that are designed to withstand the elements. This means fixtures that are waterproof and resistant to rust and corrosion.
  • Install lighting fixtures in strategic locations around your summerhouse. You should have lights near the entrance, on the deck or patio, and in any other areas where you plan to spend time.
  • Use a variety of lighting fixtures to create a layered lighting effect. This could include overhead lights, wall sconces, and table lamps.
  • Consider using dimmable lights so that you can adjust the brightness of the lighting to create the desired ambiance.

By incorporating outdoor lighting into your summerhouse, you can create a space that is both stylish and functional, and that can be enjoyed day and night.

With a little planning and effort, you can create a summerhouse that is the perfect place to relax, entertain, and enjoy the outdoors.

Create a focal point

Every room needs a focal point, and your summerhouse is no exception. A focal point is an object or area that draws the eye and creates a sense of interest. It can be anything from a fireplace to a piece of art to a beautiful view.

  • Choose a focal point that reflects the style of your summerhouse. If you have a rustic summerhouse, you might choose a stone fireplace as your focal point. If you have a more modern summerhouse, you might choose a piece of abstract art.

  • Use lighting to highlight your focal point. This could be done with a spotlight, a table lamp, or even a string of lights.
