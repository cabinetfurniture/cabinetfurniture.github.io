French Polishing

French polishing is a traditional technique used to create a high-gloss finish on wood furniture. It is a time-consuming and labor-intensive process, but it produces beautiful and durable results.

  • Traditional French Polishing: This is the most traditional method of French polishing, and it uses a shellac-based finish. Shellac is a natural resin that is dissolved in alcohol, and it is applied to the wood in thin layers. Each layer is then polished with a rubbing pad to create a smooth, glossy finish.
  • Modern French Polishing: Modern French polishing uses a variety of different finishes, including polyurethane and lacquer. These finishes are more durable than shellac, and they can be applied more quickly. However, they do not produce the same depth of gloss as traditional French polishing.
  • French Polishing Repairs: We can repair all types of French polishing damage, including scratches, dents, and water damage. We use traditional French polishing techniques to ensure that the repairs are invisible.
  • French Polishing Restoration: We can restore your French polished furniture to its original condition. This includes repairing any damage, repolishing the finish, and replacing any missing or damaged parts.
