Question 1: What is the best material for garden furniture?
There are a few different materials that are commonly used for garden furniture, each with its own advantages and disadvantages. Some of the most popular materials include:

  • Teak: Teak is a durable hardwood that is naturally resistant to rot and decay. It is also very easy to care for, requiring only occasional cleaning with soap and water.
  • Cedar: Cedar is another durable hardwood that is naturally resistant to rot and decay. It is also very lightweight and easy to move around.
  • Wrought iron: Wrought iron is a strong and durable metal that is resistant to rust and corrosion. It is also very heavy, which can make it difficult to move around.
  • Aluminum: Aluminum is a lightweight and durable metal that is resistant to rust and corrosion. It is also very easy to care for, requiring only occasional cleaning with soap and water.
  • Plastic: Plastic is a lightweight and inexpensive material that is available in a variety of colors and styles. However, it is not as durable as other materials and can be damaged by UV rays.

  • The style of your garden: Choose furniture that matches the style of your garden. For example, if you have a traditional garden, you might want to choose furniture made from teak or cedar. If you have a modern garden, you might want to choose furniture made from aluminum or plastic.
  • Your budget: Garden furniture can range in price from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. Set a budget before you start shopping so that you don't overspend.
