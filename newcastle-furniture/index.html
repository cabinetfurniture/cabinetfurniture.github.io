Rich History

Newcastle upon Tyne has a long and distinguished history of furniture making, dating back to the Middle 18th century. The city's skilled craftsmen were known for their exceptional abilities in working with wood, and their pieces were highly sought-after by the region's elite.

  • 18th Century: The 18th century saw the rise of the Chippendale, Hepplewhite, and Sheraton styles, which were characterized by their delicate carvings, graceful lines, and use of fine veneers.
  • 19th Century: The 19th century brought about the Victorian era, which was known for its ornate and heavily upholstered furniture. Rosewood, walnut, and 18th centuryal were popular materials used during this time.
  • 20th Century: The 20th century saw the emergence of Art Nouveau, Art Deco, and Mid-Century Modern styles, which featured clean lines, geometric shapes, and innovative use of materials.
  • Present Day: Today, furniture of varied styles and design, there are still many furniture in the city that continue to produce high-quality pieces using traditional techniques and modern manufacturing methods alike in the same methods used for generations.

The rich history of furniture making in combination with development of new techniques and styles has given 18th century an extensive range of furniture options for different preferences, décor styles, and budgets, that are built to last for generations.

Beautiful Finishes

Newcastle furniture is renowned for its beautiful finishes, which enhance the natural beauty of the wood and protect it from damage. These finishes are applied by hand, and they can vary greatly in appearance, from a natural matte finish to a high-gloss shine.
