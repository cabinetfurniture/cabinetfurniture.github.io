Sell Old Furniture For Extra Cash

Written by Petter Apr 18, 2024 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking for a way to declutter your home and make some extra money? Selling your old furniture is a great option. There are many different ways to sell old furniture, so you can find the method that works best for you. In this article, we will go over some tips for selling old furniture fast and review some popular online marketplaces.

When selling old furniture, first impressions are very important. Clean your furniture thoroughly before taking pictures. Good lighting and a clean background will help your furniture look its best. Avoid taking pictures in direct sunlight, as this can cause glare. Instead, try taking pictures in natural light near a window. Open some curtains or blinds to let some light in.

Now that you have your furniture looking its best, it's time to start selling! There are many different online marketplaces where you can sell your old furniture. Some popular options include eBay, Facebook Marketplace, and Craigslist.

Clean and repair

Before you sell your old furniture, it's important to clean and repair it to make it more appealing to buyers. A little bit of elbow grease can go a long way in increasing the value of your furniture.

  • Clean the furniture thoroughly

    Use a mild soap and water solution to clean the furniture. Be sure to get into all the nooks and crannies. If the furniture is upholstered, vacuum it to remove any dust or pet hair.

  • Repair any damage

    If the furniture has any scratches or dents, you can repair them yourself using a furniture repair kit. If the damage is more severe, you may need to take the furniture to a professional repair shop.

  • Refinish the furniture (optional)

    If the furniture is looking a bit tired, you can refinish it to give it a new lease on life. You can paint the furniture, stain it, or apply a new finish. Refinishing furniture can be a bit time-consuming, but it can be a great way to increase the value of your furniture.

  • Stage the furniture

    Once you have cleaned and repaired the furniture, stage it to make it more appealing to buyers. Arrange the furniture in a way that shows off its best features. You can also add some decorative items, such as pillows, throws, or plants, to make the furniture look more inviting.

By following these tips, you can clean and repair your old furniture to make it more appealing to buyers and increase its value.

Take good pictures

Taking good pictures is one of the most important things you can do to sell your old furniture quickly and for a good price. Here are a few tips:

Use good lighting. Natural light is always best, so try to take pictures near a window. If you're taking pictures indoors, use a lamp or overhead light to brighten the area.

Take pictures from multiple angles. This will give buyers a good sense of the furniture's size and condition. Be sure to take pictures of any damage or wear and tear.

Use a high-quality camera. A good camera will take clear, sharp pictures that will make your furniture look its best. If you don't have a good camera, you can use your smartphone, but be sure to use the highest quality setting.

Edit your pictures. Once you've taken your pictures, you can edit them to make them look even better. You can crop the pictures to remove any unwanted background, and you can adjust the brightness and contrast to make the furniture look its best.

By following these tips, you can take good pictures of your old furniture that will help you sell it quickly and for a good price.

Write a detailed description

In addition to taking good pictures, you should also write a detailed description of your old furniture. The description should include the following information:

The type of furniture. What type of furniture is it? Is it a couch, a chair, a table, etc.?

The dimensions. How big is the furniture? Include the length, width, and height.

The condition. Is the furniture in good condition, fair condition, or poor condition? Be honest about the condition of the furniture, but also highlight any positive features.

The materials. What is the furniture made of? Is it wood, metal, plastic, etc.?

The style. What is the style of the furniture? Is it modern, traditional, contemporary, etc.?

The color. What color is the furniture?

Any other relevant information. Is there anything else that buyers should know about the furniture? For example, does it come with any accessories, such as cushions or pillows?

By writing a detailed description, you can help buyers make an informed decision about whether or not to purchase your furniture.

Set a fair price

Setting a fair price for your old furniture is important. If you price it too high, buyers will be less likely to purchase it. If you price it too low, you'll end up losing money. Here are a few tips for setting a fair price:

  • Research comparable prices. The best way to set a fair price is to research comparable prices. Look at other listings for similar furniture on online marketplaces, such as eBay, Facebook Marketplace, and Craigslist. This will give you a good idea of what other people are selling their furniture for.
  • Consider the condition of your furniture. If your furniture is in good condition, you can price it higher than furniture that is in poor condition. However, be honest about the condition of your furniture. Don't try to pass off damaged furniture as being in good condition.
  • Factor in the cost of shipping (if applicable). If you're selling your furniture online, you'll need to factor in the cost of shipping. This can vary depending on the size and weight of the furniture, as well as the distance it needs to be shipped.
  • Be willing to negotiate. Most buyers will expect to negotiate the price of your furniture. Be prepared to come down on your price, but don't be afraid to stand your ground if you think your price is fair.

By following these tips, you can set a fair price for your old furniture that will help you sell it quickly and for a good price.

Choose the right selling platform

There are many different online marketplaces where you can sell your old furniture. Each platform has its own advantages and disadvantages. Here is a brief overview of some of the most popular selling platforms:

eBay is a popular online auction site where you can sell almost anything. eBay has a large audience of potential buyers, but the fees can be high. You'll also need to deal with shipping the furniture yourself.

Facebook Marketplace is a great option if you're looking to sell your furniture locally. Facebook Marketplace is free to use and it's easy to list your furniture. However, the audience is more limited than on eBay.

Craigslist is another popular option for selling furniture locally. Craigslist is free to use and it's easy to list your furniture. However, Craigslist can be a bit more risky than other platforms, as there is less buyer protection.

Chairish is a specialized online marketplace for buying and selling used furniture. Chairish has a more upscale audience than other platforms, so you can expect to get a higher price for your furniture. However, Chairish takes a 20% commission on all sales.

When choosing a selling platform, it's important to consider the following factors:

  • The size and condition of your furniture
  • Your budget
  • Your timeline for selling your furniture

Be prepared to negotiate

Most buyers will expect to negotiate the price of your furniture. This is especially true if you're selling your furniture online. Here are a few tips for negotiating with buyers:

  • Be realistic about your price. Don't set your price too high, or buyers will be less likely to negotiate. However, don't be afraid to stand your ground if you think your price is fair.
  • Be prepared to come down on your price. Most buyers will expect you to come down on your price, so be prepared to do so. However, don't be afraid to say no if the buyer's offer is too low.
  • Be willing to compromise. Negotiation is all about compromise. Be willing to meet the buyer halfway, but don't be afraid to walk away from the deal if you're not happy with the price.
  • Be patient. Negotiation can take time. Don't get discouraged if the buyer doesn't accept your first offer. Be patient and keep negotiating until you reach an agreement.

By following these tips, you can be prepared to negotiate with buyers and sell your old furniture for a fair price.
