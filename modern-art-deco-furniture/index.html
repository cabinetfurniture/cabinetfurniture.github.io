Modern Art Deco Furniture

Art Deco style combines geometric shapes, bold colors, and luxurious materials to create a glamorous and sophisticated look. Here are ten important points about modern art deco furniture:

  • Geometric shapes
  • Bold colors
  • Luxurious materials
  • Streamlined silhouettes
  • Ornate details
  • Exotic influences
  • High-contrast finishes
  • Functionality and style
  • Art Deco revival
  • Investment pieces

Modern art deco furniture is a great way to add a touch of glamour and sophistication to your home. It is also a great investment, as well-made pieces can last for many years.

Geometric shapes

Geometric shapes are one of the most important elements of modern art deco furniture. Art deco designers used geometric shapes to create a sense of order and symmetry, and to emphasize the clean lines and streamlined silhouettes that are characteristic of the style. Common geometric shapes used in art deco furniture include circles, squares, rectangles, and triangles.

Circles and squares are often used to create a sense of balance and harmony. Rectangles and triangles can be used to add a sense of movement and dynamism to a piece of furniture. Geometric shapes can also be used to create optical illusions, such as the illusion of depth or movement.

In addition to their aesthetic appeal, geometric shapes also serve a functional purpose in art deco furniture. For example, circles and squares can be used to create strong and durable joints, while triangles can be used to add support to a piece of furniture.

Overall, geometric shapes are an essential element of modern art deco furniture. They add a sense of order, symmetry, and sophistication to the style, and they can also be used to create optical illusions and add support to a piece of furniture.

Bold colors

Bold colors are another important element of modern art deco furniture. Art deco designers used bold colors to create a sense of drama and excitement, and to emphasize the geometric shapes that are characteristic of the style. Common bold colors used in art deco furniture include black, white, red, blue, green, and yellow.

Black:

Black is a classic art deco color that adds a sense of sophistication and drama to a piece of furniture. It can be used to create a bold statement or to add a touch of contrast to a lighter-colored piece of furniture.

White:

White is another popular art deco color that adds a sense of light and airiness to a piece of furniture. It can be used to create a clean and minimalist look, or it can be paired with bolder colors to create a more dramatic effect.

Red:

Red is a bold and vibrant color that adds a sense of excitement and energy to a piece of furniture. It can be used to create a focal point in a room, or it can be used to add a touch of color to a more neutral-colored piece of furniture.

Blue:

Blue is a calming and serene color that adds a sense of peace and tranquility to a piece of furniture. It can be used to create a relaxing atmosphere in a room, or it can be used to add a touch of color to a more neutral-colored piece of furniture.

Overall, bold colors are an essential element of modern art deco furniture. They add a sense of drama, excitement, and energy to the style, and they can be used to create a variety of different looks.

Luxurious materials

Art deco furniture is often made from luxurious materials, such as mahogany, ebony, walnut, and marble. These materials add a sense of opulence and sophistication to the style, and they help to create a sense of drama and excitement. In addition to these traditional materials, art deco designers also used new materials, such as chrome and glass, to create a modern and futuristic look.

  • Mahogany:

    Mahogany is a rich and luxurious wood that is often used to make art deco furniture. It is a durable wood that is resistant to scratches and dents, and it has a beautiful reddish-brown color. Mahogany furniture is often carved with intricate designs, and it can be inlaid with other materials, such as ivory or metal.

  • Ebony:

    Ebony is a dark and exotic wood that is often used to make art deco furniture. It is a very hard and durable wood, and it has a beautiful black color. Ebony furniture is often simple and elegant, and it can be inlaid with other materials, such as ivory or metal.

  • Walnut:

    Walnut is a medium-brown wood that is often used to make art deco furniture. It is a strong and durable wood, and it has a beautiful grain pattern. Walnut furniture is often carved with intricate designs, and it can be inlaid with other materials, such as ivory or metal.

  • Marble:

    Marble is a beautiful and luxurious stone that is often used to make art deco furniture. It is a durable stone that is resistant to scratches and stains, and it comes in a variety of colors and patterns. Marble furniture is often simple and elegant, and it can be inlaid with other materials, such as metal or glass.

Overall, luxurious materials are an essential element of modern art deco furniture. They add a sense of opulence and sophistication to the style, and they help to create a sense of drama and excitement.

Streamlined silhouettes

Streamlined silhouettes are another important element of modern art deco furniture. Art deco designers wanted to create furniture that was both stylish and functional, and they believed that streamlined silhouettes were the best way to achieve this goal. Streamlined silhouettes are characterized by their clean lines and lack of ornamentation. This gives art deco furniture a modern and sophisticated look, and it also makes it easy to clean and maintain.

Streamlined silhouettes can be achieved in a variety of ways. One common technique is to use geometric shapes to create the basic structure of a piece of furniture. For example, a coffee table might have a rectangular top and base, with simple legs that are also rectangular in shape. Another technique is to use curves to create a more fluid and dynamic look. For example, a chair might have a curved back and seat, with legs that are also curved.

Streamlined silhouettes are not only aesthetically pleasing, but they also have a number of functional benefits. For example, streamlined furniture is easier to move and rearrange than furniture with more bulky or ornate silhouettes. Streamlined furniture is also less likely to collect dust and dirt, which makes it easier to clean and maintain.

Overall, streamlined silhouettes are an essential element of modern art deco furniture. They give art deco furniture a modern and sophisticated look, and they also make it easy to clean and maintain.

Ornate details

Ornate details are another important element of modern art deco furniture. Art deco designers often used ornate details to add a touch of luxury and glamour to their furniture. These details can be found on all types of art deco furniture, from chairs and sofas to tables and cabinets. Common ornate details include carvings, moldings, and inlays.

Carvings are a common way to add ornate details to art deco furniture. Carvings can be found on all parts of a piece of furniture, from the legs to the arms to the back. Common carving motifs include geometric shapes, floral patterns, and animal figures.

Moldings are another common way to add ornate details to art deco furniture. Moldings can be used to create a variety of different effects, such as framing a panel or creating a decorative border. Common molding profiles include egg-and-dart, ogee, and torus.

Inlays are a third common way to add ornate details to art deco furniture. Inlays are created by inserting a different material into a recess in the surface of a piece of furniture. Common inlay materials include wood, metal, and ivory. Inlays can be used to create a variety of different patterns, such as geometric shapes, floral patterns, and animal figures.

Ornate details can add a touch of luxury and glamour to modern art deco furniture. They can also be used to create a variety of different looks, from simple and elegant to bold and dramatic.

Exotic influences

Exotic influences are another important element of modern art deco furniture. Art deco designers were fascinated by the art and culture of other countries, and they often incorporated exotic influences into their furniture designs. These influences can be seen in the use of exotic materials, such as bamboo, lacquer, and ivory, as well as in the use of exotic motifs, such as animal prints and geometric patterns.

One of the most common exotic influences in art deco furniture is the use of bamboo. Bamboo is a strong and durable material that can be used to create a variety of different furniture pieces, such as chairs, tables, and screens. Bamboo furniture often has a simple and elegant look, and it can be stained or painted to create a variety of different colors and finishes.

Another common exotic influence in art deco furniture is the use of lacquer. Lacquer is a type of paint that is made from the sap of the lacquer tree. Lacquer is often used to create a high-gloss finish on furniture, and it can be decorated with a variety of different designs, such as floral patterns and geometric shapes.

Ivory is another exotic material that is often used in art deco furniture. Ivory is a hard and durable material that is often used to create small objects, such as figurines and jewelry. Ivory can also be used to inlay furniture, and it can be used to create a variety of different patterns and designs.

Overall, exotic influences are an important element of modern art deco furniture. These influences can be seen in the use of exotic materials, such as bamboo, lacquer, and ivory, as well as in the use of exotic motifs, such as animal prints and geometric patterns.

Functionality and style

Functionality and style are two important considerations when choosing modern art deco furniture. Art deco furniture is known for its stylish designs, but it is also important to choose pieces that are functional and comfortable.   • Choose pieces that are comfortable: Art deco furniture can be stylish, but it is also important to choose pieces that are comfortable. If you are going to be spending a lot of time sitting in a chair or on a sofa, make sure that it is comfortable to sit in. Test out the furniture before you buy it to make sure that it is the right size and shape for you.
  • Consider your lifestyle: When choosing art deco furniture, it is important to consider your lifestyle. If you have a lot of guests, you may want to choose pieces that are easy to clean and maintain. If you have pets, you may want to choose pieces that are made from durable materials that can withstand scratches and stains.
  • Mix and match different styles: Art deco furniture can be mixed and matched with other styles to create a unique and personal look. For example, you could pair an art deco chair with a modern coffee table or a vintage rug. Experiment with different combinations to find a look that you love.

By following these tips, you can choose art deco furniture that is both stylish and functional. With its clean lines, bold colors, and luxurious materials, art deco furniture can add a touch of glamour and sophistication to any home.

Art deco furniture has experienced a revival in popularity in recent years. Art deco furniture is also seen as a good investment, as it is likely to appreciate in value over time.

The Art Deco revival has led to a renewed interest in the original art deco furniture from the 1920s and 1930s. However, there is also a growing market for new art deco furniture that is inspired by the original designs. This new art deco furniture is often made using modern materials and techniques, but it still retains the classic art deco look.

The Art Deco revival is a great opportunity to add a touch of glamour and sophistication to your home. Whether you choose original art deco furniture or new art deco furniture inspired by the original designs, you are sure to find pieces that you will love for years to come.
