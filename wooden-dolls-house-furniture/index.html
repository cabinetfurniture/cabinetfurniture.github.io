FAQ

Have questions about garden furniture? Check out our frequently asked questions (FAQs) to find answers to some of the most common inquiries.

Question 1: What is the best material for garden furniture?
Answer 1: The best material for garden furniture depends on your specific needs and preferences. Some popular materials include:

  • Wood: Wood is a classic choice for garden furniture, offering a natural and timeless look. However, it requires regular maintenance to protect it from the elements.
  • Metal: Metal furniture is durable and weather-resistant, making it a good choice for areas with harsh weather conditions. However, it can get hot in direct sunlight.
  • Plastic: Plastic furniture is lightweight, affordable, and easy to maintain. However, it may not be as durable as wood or metal.
  • Wicker: Wicker furniture is made from natural materials and has a unique, textured look. However, it may require more care and maintenance than other materials.

Question 2: How do I choose the right size garden furniture?
Answer 2: When choosing the right size garden furniture, consider the following factors:

  • Space available: Make sure to measure the available space in your garden before purchasing furniture. You don't want to overcrowd the area or make it difficult to move around.
  • Number of people: Think about how many people you typically entertain in your garden. Choose furniture that can comfortably seat everyone.
  • Intended use: Consider how you plan to use the furniture. If you want to relax and lounge, choose comfortable chairs and a coffee table. If you plan to dine outdoors, choose a dining table and chairs.

Question 3: How do I care for my garden furniture?
Answer 3: The best way to care for your garden furniture depends on the material it is made from. Here are some general tips:

  • Wood: Clean wood furniture with a mild detergent and water solution. Apply a sealant or protectant to protect the wood from the elements.
  • Metal: Clean metal furniture with a mild detergent and water solution. Rinse thoroughly and dry completely to prevent rust.
  • Plastic: Clean plastic furniture with a mild detergent and water solution. Rinse thoroughly and dry completely.
  • Wicker: Clean wicker furniture with a soft brush or vacuum cleaner. Avoid using harsh chemicals or water, as this can damage the wicker.

Question 6: Can I leave my garden furniture outside all year round?
Answer 6: Whether or not you can leave your garden furniture outside all year round depends on the material it is made from and the climate in your area. If you live in a harsh climate with extreme temperatures or heavy rainfall, it is best to store your garden furniture indoors or cover it with a protective cover.
