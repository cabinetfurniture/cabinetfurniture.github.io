Tips

Here are a few tips for choosing and caring for oak kitchen furniture:

Tip 1: Choose the right type of oak. There are two main types of oak used for furniture: red oak and white oak. Red oak is more common and has a reddish-brown color. White oak is less common and has a lighter color. White oak is also more resistant to rot and decay than red oak.

Tip 2: Consider the finish. Oak kitchen furniture can be finished in a variety of ways, including clear finishes, stains, and paints. A clear finish will allow you to see the natural beauty of the wood, while a stain can add a touch of color. Paint can be used to create a more custom look.

Tip 3: Protect your furniture. Oak kitchen furniture is durable, but it is still important to protect it from scratches, dents, and stains. Use coasters under glasses and plates, and place placemats under hot dishes. You can also apply a wood sealant to help protect the finish.
