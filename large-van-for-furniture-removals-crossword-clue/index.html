FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using a large van for transporting garden furniture:

Question 1: What size van do I need for my garden furniture?
Answer: The size of van you need will depend on the amount and size of your garden furniture. Measure your furniture and choose a van with a cargo area that is slightly larger to ensure a comfortable fit.

Question 2: How do I protect my garden furniture during transit?
Answer: Use blankets, towels, or bubble wrap to protect your furniture from scratches and damage. Secure the furniture with straps or bungee cords to prevent it from moving during transit.

Question 3: Can I transport wet garden furniture in a van?
Answer: Avoid transporting wet garden furniture in a van, as this can cause mold and mildew. Allow the furniture to dry completely before loading it into the van.

Question 4: What should I do if my garden furniture is too large for the van?
Answer: If your garden furniture is too large to fit in the van, consider disassembling it into smaller pieces. Alternatively, you can rent a larger van or hire a professional moving company.

Question 5: How do I load heavy garden furniture into the van?
Answer: Use a ramp or furniture dolly to load heavy garden furniture into the van. This will help to prevent injuries and damage to the furniture.

Question 6: Can I transport garden furniture in a van during bad weather?
Answer: If possible, avoid transporting garden furniture in a van during bad weather, such as heavy rain or snow. If you must transport furniture during bad weather, cover it with a tarp or plastic sheeting to protect it from the elements.

Tips

Here are some practical tips for transporting garden furniture in a van:

Tip 1: Plan ahead

Measure your garden furniture and choose a van that is the appropriate size. Ensure that you have all the necessary packing materials, such as blankets, towels, and straps.

Tip 2: Prepare your furniture

Clean your garden furniture and allow it to dry completely before packing it into the van. Disassemble any furniture that can be taken apart, such as tables and chairs.

Tip 3: Protect your furniture

Use blankets, towels, or bubble wrap to protect your furniture from scratches and damage. Secure the furniture with straps or bungee cords to prevent it from moving during transit.

Tip 4: Load the van carefully

Load the heaviest items first, and distribute the weight evenly throughout the van. Use a ramp or furniture dolly to load heavy furniture, and secure all items with straps or bungee cords.
