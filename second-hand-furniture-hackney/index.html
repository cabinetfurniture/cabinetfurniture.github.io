Treasure hunt in local markets.

Hackney's vibrant markets are a treasure trove of second-hand furniture, offering a unique and exciting shopping experience. From bustling street markets to charming indoor markets, there's something for every taste and budget.

  • Columbia Road Flower Market:

    Every Sunday, Columbia Road transforms into a bustling market, where you can find an eclectic mix of second-hand furniture, vintage clothing, and beautiful flowers. Keep an eye out for hidden gems among the stalls, as you never know what you might discover.

  • Hackney Flea Market:

    Held on the last Sunday of every month, the Hackney Flea Market is a haven for bargain hunters and vintage enthusiasts. With a wide range of furniture, homeware, and clothing on offer, you're sure to find unique pieces to add character to your home.

  • Chatsworth Road Market:

    Located in the heart of Hackney, Chatsworth Road Market is a vibrant street market with a diverse selection of second-hand furniture. From antique pieces to mid-century modern finds, there's something for every style and budget. Be sure to haggle with the vendors for the best prices.

  • Broadway Market:

    Known for its delicious street food and independent shops, Broadway Market also boasts a small but charming selection of second-hand furniture stalls. Browse through vintage furniture, upcycled pieces, and quirky homeware to find unique items that will add personality to your space.

Remember to arrive early to get the best selection and be prepared to rummage through piles of furniture to find the hidden gems. Don't forget to bring cash as many vendors may not accept card payments.

Explore independent furniture stores.

Hackney is home to a thriving community of independent furniture stores, each offering a unique and carefully curated selection of second-hand furniture. These stores are a great place to find high-quality, one-of-a-kind pieces that will add character and charm to your home.

Some of our favorite independent furniture stores in Hackney include:

  • The Hackney Furniture Company:

    This charming store specializes in mid-century modern furniture and lighting. With a focus on quality and sustainability, The Hackney Furniture Company offers a carefully selected range of vintage and upcycled pieces.

  • The Old Cinema:

    Housed in a former cinema, this eclectic store offers a mix of vintage furniture, industrial pieces, and upcycled treasures. The Old Cinema is a great place to find unique and eye-catching items that will make a statement in your home.

  • Revival Retro:

    As the name suggests, Revival Retro specializes in retro and vintage furniture from the 1950s to the 1980s. From colorful Formica tables to sleek Danish modern chairs, Revival Retro has something for every lover of vintage style.

  • Hackney Wick Furniture:

    Located in the vibrant Hackney Wick area, this store offers a diverse selection of second-hand furniture, upcycled pieces, and handmade items. Hackney Wick Furniture is a great place to find unique and affordable furniture that reflects the creative spirit of the area.

When exploring independent furniture stores in Hackney, be sure to take your time and browse through the entire selection. You never know what hidden gems you might discover! And don't be afraid to ask the store owners questions about the history and provenance of the pieces. They are often happy to share their knowledge and help you find the perfect piece for your home.

Upcycle pre-loved pieces.

Upcycling is a great way to transform old, unwanted furniture into stylish and unique pieces that reflect your personal style. With a little creativity and some simple DIY skills, you can upcycle almost any piece of furniture into something new and exciting.

  • Repaint or reupholster:

    One of the easiest ways to upcycle furniture is to repaint or reupholster it. This is a great way to give a piece of furniture a fresh new look and make it match your existing dÃ©cor. You can find a wide variety of paints and fabrics specifically designed for furniture at most home improvement stores.

  • Add new hardware:

    Another simple way to upcycle furniture is to replace the hardware. New knobs, handles, or drawer pulls can give a piece of furniture a whole new look and feel. You can find a wide variety of hardware options at most home improvement stores and online retailers.

  • Repurpose furniture:

    Sometimes, the best way to upcycle furniture is to repurpose it. For example, an old dresser can be turned into a media console, a nightstand can be turned into a side table, or a coffee table can be turned into a bench. With a little creativity, the possibilities are endless.

  • Combine different pieces:

    Another fun way to upcycle furniture is to combine different pieces to create something new. For example, you could combine an old table base with a new tabletop to create a unique dining table. Or, you could combine two old chairs to create a loveseat.

Upcycling furniture is a great way to be creative, reduce waste, and save money. With a little effort, you can transform old, unwanted furniture into stylish and unique pieces that you'll love for years to come.

Shop sustainably and affordably.

  • Support the circular economy:

    When you buy second-hand furniture, you are supporting the circular economy. The circular economy is a model of production and consumption that aims to eliminate waste and pollution by keeping resources in use for as long as possible. By buying used furniture, you are helping to keep furniture out of landfills and you are also creating a demand for second-hand furniture, which encourages businesses to sell more used furniture.

  • Save money:

    Second-hand furniture is often much cheaper than new furniture. This is because the cost of materials and labor has already been paid for. You can often find high-quality second-hand furniture for a fraction of the price of new furniture.

  • Find unique and stylish pieces:

    Second-hand furniture stores are often full of unique and stylish pieces that you won't find in big box stores. This is because second-hand furniture comes from a variety of sources, including homes, businesses, and estate sales. You're sure to find something that reflects your personal style and adds character to your home.

Shopping for second-hand furniture in Hackney is a great way to shop sustainably, affordably, and stylishly. So next time you need a new piece of furniture, be sure to check out the many second-hand furniture stores in the borough.

Have questions about garden furniture? We've got answers! Here are some frequently asked questions about garden furniture, along with their answers:

Question 1: What are the different types of garden furniture?
Answer 1: Garden furniture comes in a wide variety of styles and materials, including wood, metal, plastic, and wicker. You can choose from a variety of pieces, such as chairs, tables, benches, loungers, and umbrellas.

Question 2: What material is best for garden furniture?
Answer 2: The best material for garden furniture depends on your needs and preferences. Wood is a classic choice that is both durable and stylish. Metal is another durable option that is often more affordable than wood. Plastic is a lightweight and low-maintenance option, but it may not be as durable as wood or metal. Wicker is a natural material that adds a touch of rustic charm to your garden.

Question 3: How do I choose the right garden furniture for my needs?
Answer 3: When choosing garden furniture, consider the size of your garden, the style of your home, and your personal preferences. Make sure to choose furniture that is comfortable and durable, and that will withstand the elements.

Question 4: How do I care for my garden furniture?
Answer 4: The best way to care for your garden furniture is to clean it regularly with a mild soap and water solution. You should also protect your furniture from the elements by covering it when not in use.

Question 5: Where can I find good deals on garden furniture?
Answer 5: You can find good deals on garden furniture at a variety of places, including home improvement stores, garden centers, and online retailers. You can also find used garden furniture at garage sales, thrift stores, and consignment shops.

Question 6: How can I make my garden furniture more comfortable?
Answer 6: You can make your garden furniture more comfortable by adding cushions, pillows, and throws. You can also add a rug to help define the space and make it more inviting.

Tips

Here are a few tips for choosing and caring for garden furniture to make the most of your outdoor space:

Tip 1: Choose durable materials:
Garden furniture is exposed to the elements, so it's important to choose durable materials that can withstand the sun, rain, and wind. Good choices include teak, cedar, and metal. If you choose wood furniture, make sure it is treated to protect it from rot and decay.

Tip 2: Choose comfortable furniture:
You'll be spending a lot of time relaxing in your garden furniture, so it's important to choose pieces that are comfortable. Look for chairs and loungers with good back support and cushions that are firm but not too soft. You may also want to add cushions and pillows to make your furniture even more comfortable.

Tip 3: Choose furniture that fits your needs:
Consider how you plan to use your garden furniture before you buy it. If you're planning on hosting large gatherings, you'll need furniture that can accommodate a lot of people. If you're just looking for a place to relax and enjoy the outdoors, you may be able to get away with a smaller set of furniture.

Tip 4: Care for your garden furniture:
To keep your garden furniture looking its best and lasting for many years, it's important to care for it properly. Clean your furniture regularly with a mild soap and water solution. You should also protect your furniture from the elements by covering it when not in use. If you live in a climate with harsh winters, you may want to store your furniture indoors during the winter months.

By following these tips, you can choose and care for garden furniture that will make your outdoor space a comfortable and inviting place to relax and enjoy.
