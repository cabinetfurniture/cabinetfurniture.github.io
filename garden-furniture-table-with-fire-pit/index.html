Fuel Type

The choice of fuel type for your garden furniture table with fire pit is a crucial decision that will impact the overall performance, safety, and maintenance requirements of your fire feature.

  • Propane

    Propane is a popular fuel choice for fire pit tables due to its convenience and clean-burning properties. It produces a consistent and adjustable flame, and it doesn't leave behind any ash or residue. Propane tanks are readily available at most hardware stores and home improvement centers.

  • Natural Gas

    Natural gas is another popular fuel option, especially for those who have access to a natural gas line in their backyard. Natural gas burns cleanly and efficiently, providing a steady and controllable flame. However, installation requires a qualified professional, and the availability of natural gas may vary depending on your location.

  • Wood

    Wood-burning fire pit tables offer a classic and rustic ambiance. The crackling sound of burning wood and the scent of campfire can create a cozy and inviting atmosphere. However, wood-burning fire pits require more maintenance, as you need to gather and store firewood, and clean out the ashes regularly.

  • Gel Fuel

    Gel fuel is a portable and self-contained fuel option for fire pit tables. It is made from a blend of isopropyl alcohol and gelling agents, and it burns with a clean and smokeless flame. Gel fuel is easy to use and doesn't require any special equipment, but it may not provide as much heat output as other fuel types.

Consider your individual needs and preferences when selecting the fuel type for your fire pit table. Factors to consider include the availability of fuel sources, the desired heat output, the amount of maintenance you are willing to undertake, and the overall aesthetic you want to achieve.

Heat Output

The heat output of your garden furniture table with fire pit is an important consideration, as it will determine how effectively it can warm your outdoor space. Heat output is measured in British Thermal Units (BTUs), and the higher the BTU rating, the more heat the fire pit will produce.

  • Low Heat Output (less than 50,000 BTUs)

    Fire pit tables with low heat output are suitable for small spaces or for creating a cozy and intimate ambiance. They may not provide enough heat to warm a large area, but they can extend your outdoor season by providing a gentle warmth on cool evenings.

  • Medium Heat Output (50,000 to 100,000 BTUs)

    Fire pit tables with medium heat output are ideal for mid-sized spaces and for providing warmth to a wider area. They can create a comfortable and inviting atmosphere for gatherings, allowing you to enjoy your outdoor space even on cooler nights.

  • High Heat Output (over 100,000 BTUs)

    Fire pit tables with high heat output are designed for large spaces and for providing maximum warmth. They can heat a wide area effectively, making them ideal for entertaining guests or for creating a warm and inviting outdoor living room.

  • Adjustable Heat Output

    Some fire pit tables offer adjustable heat output, allowing you to control the intensity of the flame and the amount of heat produced. This feature provides greater flexibility and allows you to customize the heat output to suit your needs and the weather conditions.

Consider the size of your outdoor space and the desired level of warmth when selecting the heat output of your fire pit table. A higher BTU rating will provide more heat, but it may also consume more fuel. Choose a fire pit table with a heat output that meets your specific requirements and allows you to enjoy your outdoor space comfortably.

Material Durability

The durability of your garden furniture table with fire pit is paramount, as it will determine how well it withstands the elements and the rigors of outdoor use. Different materials offer varying levels of durability, and choosing the right material for your specific needs is essential.

Metal fire pit tables are known for their exceptional durability and resistance to rust and corrosion. They are typically made from steel or aluminum, which can withstand high temperatures and harsh weather conditions. Metal fire pit tables are also relatively easy to clean and maintain.

Cast iron fire pit tables are another durable option, offering a classic and rustic aesthetic. Cast iron is heavy and sturdy, providing excellent stability and heat retention. However, cast iron can be prone to rust if not properly maintained.

Concrete fire pit tables are becoming increasingly popular due to their durability and versatility. Concrete is a durable material that can withstand extreme temperatures and weather conditions. It can also be molded into various shapes and designs, offering a wide range of style options.

Wicker fire pit tables combine durability with a touch of natural elegance. Wicker is a woven material typically made from resin or natural fibers. Resin wicker is highly resistant to moisture and fading, making it a good choice for outdoor use. Natural wicker, on the other hand, requires more maintenance and protection from the elements.

Consider the climate in your area, the frequency of use, and your personal style preferences when selecting the material for your fire pit table. Choose a material that is durable enough to withstand the outdoor elements and that complements the overall design of your outdoor space.

Safety Features

Safety should be a top priority when choosing a garden furniture table with fire pit. Look for fire pit tables that incorporate the following safety features:

Ignition System: Choose a fire pit table with an electronic ignition system rather than a manual ignition system. Electronic ignition systems are safer and more convenient, as they eliminate the need for open flames or matches. Some fire pit tables also offer remote control ignition, providing added convenience.

Flame Guard: A flame guard helps to prevent embers and sparks from escaping the fire pit, reducing the risk of burns or fire hazards. Look for fire pit tables with flame guards that are made from durable materials, such as stainless steel or cast iron.

Burner Cover: A burner cover is essential for protecting the burner from the elements when the fire pit is not in use. It also helps to prevent debris from falling into the burner, which can lead to blockages or safety hazards.

CSA Certification: Look for fire pit tables that are CSA certified. CSA certification indicates that the fire pit has met rigorous safety standards and has been tested and approved by a recognized safety organization.

By choosing a fire pit table with these safety features, you can help ensure the safety of your family and guests while enjoying the warmth and ambiance of an outdoor fire.
