Furniture glides, also known as furniture sliders, are small, often unsung heroes that play a crucial role in the smooth functioning of your furniture. These seemingly insignificant components are attached to the bottom of furniture legs, allowing for easy gliding across various floor surfaces, be it hardwood, carpet, or tile.

Delving deeper into the world of furniture glides, we'll explore the different types available, their benefits, and how to choose the right ones for your furniture and flooring. We'll also provide practical tips for installation and maintenance to ensure your furniture glides remain effective and long-lasting.

Dampening vibrations: When furniture is moved across a hard surface, it creates vibrations that travel through the floor and can be heard as noise. Furniture glides absorb these vibrations, preventing them from traveling through the floor and reducing the noise level.

Reducing friction: Furniture glides reduce friction between furniture legs and the floor, allowing furniture to move more smoothly and quietly. This is especially important on hard surfaces like tile or hardwood, where furniture movement can create a loud scraping sound.

Eliminating squeaks: Furniture glides can also help eliminate squeaking noises caused by furniture movement. These noises are often caused by friction between furniture legs and the floor, or between different parts of the furniture itself. Variety of types

Furniture glides come in a variety of types, each with its own unique features and benefits. Here are some of the most common types:

  • Felt glides:

    Felt glides are made of a soft, dense material that provides excellent protection for hard floors. They are also very quiet, making them a good choice for furniture that is moved frequently.

  • Plastic glides:

    Plastic glides are a durable and affordable option. They are available in a variety of shapes and sizes, making them suitable for a wide range of furniture items. Plastic glides are also easy to install and remove.

  • Metal glides:

    Metal glides are the most durable type of furniture glide. They are often used on heavy furniture, such as sofas and dressers. Metal glides are also available in a variety of finishes, so you can choose a style that matches your furniture.

  • Roller glides:

    Roller glides have a small wheel or roller attached to the bottom. This allows furniture to move more easily, even on thick carpets or uneven surfaces. Roller glides are a good choice for furniture that is moved frequently, such as office chairs or dining chairs.

With so many different types of furniture glides available, you're sure to find the perfect ones for your furniture and flooring.

Material-specific

When choosing furniture glides, it's important to consider the material of your flooring. Different types of flooring require different types of glides to ensure optimal performance and protection.

  • Hardwood floors:

    For hardwood floors, felt glides or soft plastic glides are the best choice. These glides will protect your floors from scratches and dents.

  • Laminate floors:

    Laminate floors are also best suited for felt glides or soft plastic glides. Avoid hard plastic or metal glides, as these can scratch the laminate surface.

  • Carpet:

    For carpet, you can use any type of furniture glide. However, roller glides are a good choice because they make it easy to move furniture across the carpet.

  • Tile:

    For tile floors, felt glides or soft plastic glides are a good choice. Avoid hard plastic or metal glides, as these can scratch the tile surface.

By choosing the right furniture glides for your flooring material, you can protect your floors and keep your furniture moving smoothly.

Easy installation

Furniture glides are incredibly easy to install, making them a DIY project that anyone can handle.

  • Clean the furniture legs:

    Before installing furniture glides, make sure to clean the furniture legs to remove any dirt or debris. This will ensure a secure bond between the glides and the furniture legs.

  • Measure and mark the placement:

    Use a pencil to mark the placement of the furniture glides on the furniture legs. Make sure the marks are evenly spaced and centered on the legs.

  • Apply adhesive or screws:

    Depending on the type of furniture glides you have, you will either need to apply adhesive or insert screws. If using adhesive, apply a small amount to the back of each furniture glide and press it firmly onto the marked spot. If using screws, insert them through the holes in the furniture glides and into the furniture legs.

  • Allow the adhesive to dry:

    If you are using adhesive, allow it to dry completely before moving the furniture. This will ensure a strong bond between the glides and the furniture legs.

With just a few simple steps, you can easily install furniture glides and enjoy the benefits of smooth furniture movement and floor protection.
