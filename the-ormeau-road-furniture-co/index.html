Traditional techniques

At The Ormeau Road Furniture Co., traditional techniques are not just a nod to the past; they are the foundation of their furniture-making process. One of the most distinctive traditional techniques used by The Ormeau Road Furniture Co. is dovetail joinery. This method involves interlocking pieces of wood with precisely cut, wedge-shaped joints. If you're considering purchasing garden furniture, you may have some questions. Here are some frequently asked questions and answers to help you make an informed decision:

Question 1: What materials are best for garden furniture?

Answer 1: The best materials for garden furniture depend on your specific needs and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Wood: Wood is a classic choice for garden furniture, offering a natural and timeless look. However, it requires regular maintenance to protect it from the elements.
  • Metal: Metal furniture is durable and easy to maintain, making it a good choice for high-traffic areas. However, it can be prone to rust if not properly cared for.
  • Plastic: Plastic furniture is lightweight and affordable, making it a popular choice for casual outdoor dining and seating. However, it can be less durable than other materials.
  • Wicker: Wicker furniture is made from natural materials, such as rattan or bamboo, and offers a unique and stylish look. However, it requires regular maintenance to protect it from moisture and sun damage.

Question 2: How can I choose the right size garden furniture?

Answer 2: When choosing garden furniture, it's important to consider the size of your outdoor space and the number of people you need to accommodate. Measure the area where you plan to place the furniture and choose pieces that are appropriately sized.

Question 7: How can I care for my garden furniture?

Answer 7: The best way to care for your garden furniture is to follow the manufacturer's instructions. Here are some general tips:

  • Clean your furniture regularly with a mild soap and water solution.
  • Protect your furniture from the elements by covering it when not in use.
