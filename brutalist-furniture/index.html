Brutalism in Furniture A Guide to Its History Characteristics and Revival

Written by Frank Oct 31, 2023 · 12 min read

Brutalism, an architectural style known for its raw, unadorned aesthetic, emerged in the mid-20th century and had a significant impact on furniture design. Brutalism's distinctive characteristics, rooted in functionalism and a celebration of raw materials, manifested in furniture that was both striking and polarizing.

Characterized by exposed concrete, unpolished metal, and bold geometric forms, brutalist furniture exuded an industrial and austere quality. Its unpretentious nature challenged traditional notions of beauty and comfort, emphasizing functionality and durability over ornamentation.

While brutalist architecture faced criticism for its perceived harshness, brutalist furniture gained a cult following among those who appreciated its utilitarian appeal and brutalist ethos.

brutalist furniture

Brutalism in furniture design emerged as a striking and distinctive movement, characterized by its raw and utilitarian aesthetic. Here are seven important points to note about brutalist furniture:

  • Raw materials: Exposed concrete, unpolished metal, and weathered wood.
  • Geometric forms: Bold and angular shapes, often inspired by industrial architecture.
  • Functionalism: Emphasis on functionality and durability over ornamentation.
  • Rejection of traditional beauty standards: Celebrated the beauty of raw and unfinished materials.
  • Influence of architecture: Closely aligned with the brutalist architectural style.
  • Limited production: Often produced in small batches or as one-of-a-kind pieces.
  • Cult following: Gained a dedicated following among those who appreciate its utilitarian appeal and brutalist ethos.

Brutalism in furniture design remains a polarizing style, but its unique aesthetic and emphasis on functionality continue to inspire and influence contemporary furniture design.

Raw materials: Exposed concrete, unpolished metal, and weathered wood.

Brutalism in furniture design is characterized by its use of raw and unfinished materials, such as exposed concrete, unpolished metal, and weathered wood. These materials lend a sense of authenticity and industrial chic to brutalist furniture pieces.

Exposed concrete is a common material in brutalist architecture, and it has also been used to create striking furniture pieces. Concrete's rough texture and gray color give it a raw and unfinished look that is in keeping with the brutalist aesthetic. Concrete furniture is also incredibly durable, making it well-suited for both indoor and outdoor use.

Unpolished metal is another popular material for brutalist furniture. Metal's industrial look and feel add to the utilitarian aesthetic of brutalist pieces. Unpolished metal also has a natural patina that develops over time, which gives each piece a unique character.

Weathered wood is a third common material used in brutalist furniture design. Weathered wood has a rustic and worn look that adds a sense of warmth to brutalist pieces. It is also a sustainable material, as it can be reclaimed from old buildings or salvaged from fallen trees.

The use of raw and unfinished materials in brutalist furniture design is a reflection of the movement's emphasis on functionality and durability. Brutalism celebrates the beauty of imperfection and the natural qualities of materials. As a result, brutalist furniture pieces are often one-of-a-kind and have a timeless appeal.

Functionalism: Emphasis on functionality and durability over ornamentation.

Brutalism in furniture design is characterized by its emphasis on functionality and durability over ornamentation. Brutalism rejects the idea that furniture should be solely decorative and instead focuses on creating pieces that are both useful and long-lasting.

  • Simple and practical designs: Brutalism furniture is often simple and practical in design. It is designed to be comfortable and easy to use, without any unnecessary frills or ornamentation.
  • Durable construction: Brutalism furniture is built to last. It is made from sturdy materials, such as concrete, metal, and wood, and is designed to withstand heavy use.
  • Multifunctional pieces: Brutalism furniture often serves multiple functions. For example, a coffee table may also function as a storage unit, or a chair may also function as a sculpture.
  • Rejection of traditional notions of beauty: Brutalism furniture rejects traditional notions of beauty. It celebrates the beauty of raw materials and unfinished surfaces.

Rejection of traditional beauty standards: Celebrated the beauty of raw and unfinished materials.

Brutalism in furniture design rejects traditional beauty standards and instead celebrates the beauty of raw and unfinished materials. Brutalism furniture is not designed to be polished or refined. It celebrates the natural beauty of materials such as concrete, metal, and wood.

  • Exposed surfaces: Brutalism furniture often has exposed surfaces, such as concrete walls or metal beams. These surfaces are not hidden or covered up, but are instead celebrated for their raw and unfinished beauty.
  • Imperfections: Brutalism furniture embraces imperfections. It does not attempt to hide or disguise the natural imperfections of materials. Instead, these imperfections are seen as part of the beauty of the piece.
  • Natural materials: Brutalism furniture is often made from natural materials, such as wood, stone, and metal. These materials are chosen for their natural beauty and durability.
  • Honest expression: Brutalism furniture is an honest expression of materials and construction. It does not attempt to deceive or to create an illusion of something that it is not.

The rejection of traditional beauty standards in brutalist furniture design is a reflection of the movement's commitment to authenticity and honesty. Brutalism furniture is not designed to conform to popular trends or to appeal to mass tastes. It is designed to be true to itself and to celebrate the beauty of raw materials.

Influence of architecture: Closely aligned with the brutalist architectural style.

Brutalism in furniture design is closely aligned with the brutalist architectural style. Brutalism in architecture is characterized by its use of raw materials, bold geometric forms, and emphasis on functionality. These same characteristics are also found in brutalist furniture.

  • Shared aesthetic: Brutalism furniture and brutalist architecture share a common aesthetic. Both are characterized by their use of raw materials, such as concrete, metal, and wood. Both also feature bold geometric forms and an emphasis on functionality.
  • Origins in the same era: Brutalism in furniture design and brutalism in architecture both emerged in the mid-20th century. Both movements were a reaction to the perceived excesses of modernism and a desire for a more honest and authentic approach to design.
  • Cross-pollination of ideas: There was a great deal of cross-pollination of ideas between brutalist architects and furniture designers. Many architects also designed furniture, and many furniture designers were influenced by brutalist architecture.
  • Shared commitment to functionality: Brutalism furniture and brutalist architecture share a commitment to functionality. Both types of design are designed to be useful and durable, without any unnecessary ornamentation.

The close alignment between brutalism in furniture design and brutalism in architecture is not surprising, given that both movements were born out of the same cultural and intellectual milieu. Brutalism furniture is a natural extension of brutalist architecture, and it shares the same commitment to honesty, authenticity, and functionality.

Limited production: Often produced in small batches or as one-of-a-kind pieces.

Brutalism furniture is often produced in small batches or as one-of-a-kind pieces. This is due to several factors:

High cost of production: Brutalism furniture is often made from expensive materials, such as concrete, metal, and wood. It is also often labor-intensive to produce. This makes it impractical to mass-produce brutalism furniture.

Emphasis on craftsmanship: Brutalism furniture is often made by skilled craftspeople who take pride in their work. They are not interested in mass-producing furniture, but rather in creating unique pieces that are built to last.

Limited market appeal: Brutalism furniture is not to everyone's taste. Its raw and unfinished aesthetic appeals to a niche market. This limits the demand for brutalism furniture and makes it difficult to mass-produce.

The limited production of brutalism furniture contributes to its exclusivity and desirability. It ensures that each piece is unique and special. It also allows brutalism furniture makers to focus on quality rather than quantity.
