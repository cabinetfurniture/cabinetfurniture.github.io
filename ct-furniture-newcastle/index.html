FAQ

Have questions about garden furniture? We've got you covered. Here are some frequently asked questions and their answers to help you make informed decisions when choosing and caring for your outdoor furniture.

Question 1: What materials are best for garden furniture?
Answer: The best materials for garden furniture depend on your specific needs and preferences. Wood, metal, plastic, and rattan are all popular choices. Wood offers a classic look and feel, but requires regular maintenance. Metal is durable and easy to clean, but can be prone to rust. Plastic is lightweight and low-maintenance, but may not be as durable as other materials. Rattan is a natural material that is both stylish and durable.

Question 2: How can I protect my garden furniture from the elements?
Answer: To protect your garden furniture from the elements, consider using covers or cushions when not in use. You can also apply protective sprays or sealants to help repel water and UV rays. Regular cleaning and maintenance will also help extend the life of your furniture.

Question 3: What are some tips for choosing comfortable garden furniture?
Answer: When choosing comfortable garden furniture, consider the following tips: choose pieces with supportive cushions and adjustable features, such as reclining chairs or adjustable tables. Look for furniture made from breathable materials, such as mesh or wicker, to keep you cool and comfortable on hot days. Also, consider the size and shape of the furniture to ensure it fits your space and provides ample seating.

Question 4: How can I create a cohesive look for my garden furniture?
Answer: To create a cohesive look for your garden furniture, consider choosing pieces from the same collection or in similar styles. You can also use cushions, throws, and other accessories to tie the look together. Experiment with different arrangements and layouts to find the one that works best for your space and needs.

Question 5: What are some space-saving garden furniture ideas?
Answer: If you have a small garden or patio, consider choosing space-saving garden furniture options. Look for foldable or stackable chairs and tables that can be easily stored away when not in use. Hanging chairs and benches can also be great options for maximizing space.

Question 6: Where can I find high-quality garden furniture?
Answer: You can find high-quality garden furniture at CT Furniture Newcastle. We offer a wide selection of stylish and durable garden furniture pieces to suit every taste and budget. Visit our showroom or browse our online catalog to find the perfect pieces for your outdoor space.

Tips

In addition to choosing the right garden furniture and protecting it from the elements, there are several practical tips you can follow to keep your garden furniture looking its best and lasting for years to come.

Tip 1: Clean Your Garden Furniture Regularly:

Regular cleaning is essential to maintain the appearance and longevity of your garden furniture. Use mild soap and water to clean the furniture, and rinse it thoroughly with a hose. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, as these can damage the finish of the furniture.

Tip 2: Apply Protective Treatments:

Depending on the material of your garden furniture, you may want to apply protective treatments to help repel water and UV rays. For example, you can apply a sealant to wooden furniture to protect it from moisture and fading. For metal furniture, you can use a rust-resistant coating to prevent rusting.

Tip 3: Store Your Garden Furniture Properly:

When not in use, store your garden furniture in a dry and protected place. This will help prevent damage from the elements and extend the life of your furniture. If you don't have a dedicated storage space, consider using furniture covers to protect your furniture from dust and moisture.

Tip 4: Inspect and Repair Your Garden Furniture Regularly:

Regularly inspect your garden furniture for signs of damage, such as loose screws, cracks, or rust. Make any necessary repairs promptly to prevent further damage and ensure the safety of your furniture. If you're not comfortable making repairs yourself, you can contact a professional furniture repair service.
