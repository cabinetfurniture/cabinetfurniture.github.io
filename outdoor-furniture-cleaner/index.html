Safe for all materials

When choosing an outdoor furniture cleaner, it's important to select one that is safe for all materials. This is especially important if you have furniture made from a variety of materials, such as wood, metal, plastic, or fabric.

Harsh cleaners can damage delicate materials, so it's important to choose one that is gentle enough for all surfaces. Look for a cleaner that is pH-balanced and free from harsh chemicals.

It's also important to test the cleaner on an inconspicuous area before using it on the entire piece of furniture. This will help you to ensure that the cleaner does not damage the finish.

By following these tips, you can choose an outdoor furniture cleaner that is safe for all materials and will help to keep your furniture looking its best.

Removes tough stains

One of the most important factors to consider when choosing an outdoor furniture cleaner is its ability to remove tough stains. Outdoor furniture is often exposed to a variety of stains, including food, drinks, dirt, and pollen. A good outdoor furniture cleaner will be able to remove these stains without damaging the finish of your furniture.

  • Water-based

    Water-based outdoor furniture cleaner are the most common type of cleaner. They are effective at removing a wide variety of stains, and they are generally safe for use on all materials. However, water-based cleaner can be less effective on oily stains.

  • Solvent-based

    Solvent-based outdoor furniture cleaner are more effective at removing oily stains than water-based cleaner. However, they can be more harsh on delicate materials. It is important to test solvent-based cleaner on an inconspicuous area before using it on the entire piece of furniture.

  • Enzyme-based

    Enzyme-based outdoor furniture cleaner are a good choice for removing stains from organic matter, such as food and grass. They are also gentle on delicate materials. However, enzyme-based cleaner can be more expensive than other types of cleaner.

  • Oxygen-based

    Oxygen-based outdoor furniture cleaner are a good choice for removing stains from inorganic matter, such as dirt and pollen. They are also effective at brightening fabrics. However, oxygen-based cleaner can be less effective on oily stains.

By choosing an outdoor furniture cleaner that is effective at removing tough stains, you can keep your furniture looking its best for years to come.

Protects against fading

One of the most important things you can do to protect your outdoor furniture is to choose a cleaner that contains UV protectants. UV rays from the sun can cause the color of your furniture to fade over time. A good outdoor furniture cleaner will contain UV protectants that will help to shield your furniture from the sun's harmful rays.

There are two main types of UV protectants: chemical and physical. Chemical UV protectants absorb UV rays and convert them into heat. Physical UV protectants reflect UV rays away from the surface of your furniture.

Both types of UV protectants are effective at preventing fading. However, chemical UV protectants can break down over time, so it is important to reapply them regularly. Physical UV protectants do not break down over time, so they provide long-lasting protection.

When choosing an outdoor furniture cleaner, look for one that contains both chemical and physical UV protectants. This will provide your furniture with the best possible protection against fading.

By following these tips, you can choose an outdoor furniture cleaner that will help to keep your furniture looking its best for years to come.
