Garden Furniture Preservative A Guide to Protecting and Maintaining Your Outdoor Furniture

Written by Luffy Jul 04, 2025 · 14 min read

Garden furniture is an essential part of outdoor living, providing a place to relax, dine, and entertain guests. But just like any other piece of furniture, garden furniture needs to be properly cared for in order to maintain its appearance and extend its lifespan. One of the most important ways to do this is to use a garden furniture preservative.

Garden furniture preservatives are designed to protect outdoor furniture from the elements, including sun, rain, wind, and snow. They can also help to prevent mold and mildew growth, which can damage furniture and make it unsafe to use. In addition, preservatives can help to keep furniture looking its best by preventing fading and cracking.

Now that you know why you should use a garden furniture preservative, let's take a look at how to choose the right one for your needs.

Protects from sun, rain, wind, and snow

Garden furniture is constantly exposed to the elements, which can take a toll on its appearance and lifespan. The sun's UV rays can cause fading and cracking, while rain, wind, and snow can cause warping and rotting. Garden furniture preservatives help to protect furniture from all of these elements.

  • Protects from the sun's UV rays: UV rays are the primary cause of fading and cracking in garden furniture. Preservatives contain UV inhibitors that help to block these rays and protect furniture from damage.
  • Protects from rain and moisture: Rain and moisture can cause wood furniture to warp and rot. Preservatives help to repel water and moisture, preventing damage to the furniture.
  • Protects from wind and snow: Wind and snow can be harsh on garden furniture, causing it to become scratched and damaged. Preservatives help to create a protective barrier that shields furniture from these elements.
  • Protects from extreme temperatures: Extreme temperatures, both hot and cold, can damage garden furniture. Preservatives help to protect furniture from these temperature extremes, preventing cracking and warping.

By protecting garden furniture from the elements, preservatives help to extend its lifespan and keep it looking its best.

Prevents mold and mildew growth

Mold and mildew are common problems for garden furniture, especially in humid or rainy climates. These unsightly growths can damage furniture and make it unsafe to use. Mold and mildew can also cause respiratory problems for some people.

Garden furniture preservatives contain fungicides that help to prevent the growth of mold and mildew. These fungicides work by killing mold and mildew spores and preventing them from germinating. Preservatives also help to create a protective barrier on the surface of the furniture that makes it difficult for mold and mildew to grow.

In addition to using a preservative, there are a few other things you can do to prevent mold and mildew growth on your garden furniture:

  • Keep your furniture clean. Dirt and debris can provide a food source for mold and mildew.
  • Store your furniture in a dry place when it is not in use.
  • Avoid placing your furniture in areas that are constantly wet or humid.
  • If you do notice mold or mildew growth on your furniture, clean it immediately with a mild detergent and water. You can also use a commercial mold and mildew cleaner.

By following these tips, you can help to prevent mold and mildew growth on your garden furniture and keep it looking its best.

Easy to apply

Garden furniture preservatives are typically very easy to apply. Most preservatives come in a ready-to-use formula that can be applied with a brush, roller, or sprayer. Some preservatives can even be applied with a cloth.

To apply a garden furniture preservative, simply follow these steps:

  1. Clean the furniture thoroughly to remove any dirt, debris, or mildew.
  2. Allow the furniture to dry completely.
  3. Apply the preservative according to the manufacturer's instructions. Be sure to apply the preservative evenly and to cover all surfaces of the furniture.
  4. Allow the preservative to dry completely before using the furniture.

Most garden furniture preservatives will need to be reapplied every one to two years to maintain their effectiveness. However, some preservatives may last longer, so be sure to read the manufacturer's instructions carefully.

Here are a few tips for applying garden furniture preservative:

  • Apply the preservative in a well-ventilated area.
  • Wear gloves and eye protection when applying the preservative.
  • Do not apply the preservative to furniture that is in direct sunlight.
  • Allow the preservative to dry completely before using the furniture.

By following these tips, you can easily apply garden furniture preservative and protect your furniture from the elements.

Available in a variety of finishes

Garden furniture preservatives are available in a variety of finishes, so you can choose a finish that matches the look of your furniture and your outdoor décor.

  • Clear finish: Clear finishes allow the natural beauty of the wood to show through. They are a good choice for furniture made from high-quality wood.
  • Semi-transparent finish: Semi-transparent finishes allow some of the wood grain to show through. They are a good choice for furniture made from less expensive wood or for furniture that you want to add a bit of color to.
  • Solid finish: Solid finishes completely hide the wood grain. They are a good choice for furniture that you want to change the color of or for furniture that is made from a variety of different woods.
