Material

The material of your patio small table is important because it will determine how durable and weather-proof the table is. Here are some of the most common materials used for patio small tables:

Teak: Teak is a durable hardwood that is naturally resistant to rot and decay. It is also a beautiful wood with a rich, golden brown color. Teak tables are more expensive than other types of wood tables, but they will last for many years with proper care.

Cedar: Cedar is another durable hardwood that is resistant to rot and decay. It is also a lightweight wood, making it easy to move around. Cedar tables are typically less expensive than teak tables, but they may not last as long.

Wrought iron: Wrought iron is a strong and durable material that is resistant to rust and fading. It is also a relatively lightweight material, making it easy to move around. Wrought iron tables are typically more expensive than wood tables, but they will last for many years with proper care.

Cast aluminum: Cast aluminum is a lightweight and durable material that is resistant to rust and fading. It is also a relatively inexpensive material, making it a good value for the money. Cast aluminum tables are easy to move around and require little maintenance.

When choosing a material for your patio small table, consider the climate in which you live, the amount of maintenance you are willing to do, and your budget.

Shape

The shape of your patio small table is important because it will determine how the table looks and how it functions. Here are some of the most common shapes for patio small tables:

Round: Round tables are a classic choice for patio furniture. They are space-saving and can comfortably seat a group of people. Round tables are also great for creating a cozy and intimate atmosphere.

Square: Square tables are another popular choice for patio furniture. They are sturdy and can seat a larger group of people than round tables. Square tables are also good for creating a more formal look.

Rectangular: Rectangular tables are a good choice for patios that are long and narrow. They can also be used to create a more formal look. Rectangular tables are typically larger than round or square tables, so they can seat more people.

Oval: Oval tables are a good compromise between round and rectangular tables. They are space-saving and can seat a larger group of people than round tables, but they are not as formal as rectangular tables. Oval tables are also good for creating a more intimate atmosphere.

When choosing the shape of your patio small table, consider the size and shape of your patio, the number of people you typically entertain, and the style of your patio furniture.
