Easy to apply and maintain

One of the best things about outdoor furniture oil is that it is very easy to apply and maintain. Here are the steps on how to apply outdoor furniture oil:

  1. Clean your furniture:
    Before you apply the oil, it is important to clean your furniture to remove any dirt, grime, or mildew. You can use a mild detergent and water or a commercial furniture cleaner.
  2. Sand your furniture (if necessary):
    If your furniture is very weathered or has a rough finish, you may need to sand it lightly before applying the oil. Use a fine-grit sandpaper and sand in the direction of the grain.
  3. Apply the oil:
    Apply the oil liberally to your furniture using a paintbrush, roller, or cloth. Be sure to cover all surfaces of the furniture, including the legs, arms, and back. Allow the oil to soak in for the amount of time specified on the product label.
  4. Wipe off the excess oil:
    After the oil has soaked in, wipe off any excess oil with a clean cloth. This will help to prevent the oil from becoming sticky or attracting dirt.

Once you have applied the oil, it is important to maintain it properly. Here are some tips for maintaining your outdoor furniture oil:

  • Reapply the oil regularly:
    The frequency with which you need to reapply the oil will depend on the type of oil you use and the weather conditions in your area. Generally speaking, you should reapply the oil every 3-6 months.
  • Clean your furniture regularly:
    To keep your furniture looking its best, clean it regularly with a mild detergent and water. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, as these can damage the finish.
  • Protect your furniture from the elements:
    When you are not using your furniture, protect it from the sun, rain, and wind by covering it with a tarp or storing it in a garage or shed.

By following these tips, you can keep your outdoor furniture looking its best for years to come.
