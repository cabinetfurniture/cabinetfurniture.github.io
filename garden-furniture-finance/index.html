Flexible payment plans

Garden furniture financing offers a variety of flexible payment plans that cater to different budgets and lifestyles. Whether you prefer to pay off your purchase gradually over time or make larger payments upfront, there's a plan that works for you.

  • Monthly installments: This is the most common type of payment plan, where you pay a fixed amount each month until the total purchase price is paid off. Monthly installments are typically spread over a period of 12 to 60 months, depending on the lender and the amount financed.
  • Deferred interest: With deferred interest, you can make monthly payments without paying any interest for a certain period of time, usually ranging from 6 to 24 months. This can be a great option if you need some time to get your finances in order or if you're waiting for a tax refund or other large payment.
  • Equal-pay installments: This type of payment plan divides the total purchase price into equal monthly payments, including both principal and interest. Equal-pay installments are typically shorter-term loans, often ranging from 12 to 24 months.
  • Seasonal payments: If you have a seasonal income, you may be able to find a garden furniture financing plan that allows you to make payments during your peak earning months and skip payments during your off-season.

No matter which payment plan you choose, make sure to read the terms and conditions carefully before signing the agreement. Pay attention to the interest rate, any fees associated with the loan, and the total amount you will pay over the life of the loan.

No credit history required

One of the biggest advantages of garden furniture financing is that it is often available to people with no credit history. This means that even if you are a first-time borrower or have a limited credit history, you may still be able to qualify for financing.

  • Alternative credit checks: Instead of relying solely on your credit score, some lenders may consider other factors when evaluating your application. This may include your income, employment history, and banking history.
  • No-credit-check financing: Some lenders offer no-credit-check financing, which means that they do not check your credit score at all. However, no-credit-check financing typically comes with higher interest rates and fees.
  • Co-signers: If you have no credit history or a low credit score, you may be able to get approved for garden furniture financing with a co-signer. A co-signer is someone with good credit who agrees to repay the loan if you default.
  • Rent-to-own programs: Some furniture retailers offer rent-to-own programs that allow you to rent furniture for a period of time and then purchase it at the end of the rental period. Rent-to-own programs do not typically require a credit check.

With garden furniture financing, you have options even if you have no credit history. Be sure to compare different lenders and financing options to find the one that best suits your needs and budget.
