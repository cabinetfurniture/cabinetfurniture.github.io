Consider age and maturity

When choosing teen bedroom furniture, it is also important to consider your teen's age and maturity level. Younger teens may need more assistance in choosing furniture that is appropriate for their needs, while older teens may want more say in the design of their room.

  • Younger teens (13-15 years old):

    Younger teens are still growing and developing, both physically and emotionally. They may need furniture that is adjustable or convertible, so that it can grow with them. They may also be more likely to change their interests and hobbies frequently, so it is important to choose furniture that is versatile and easy to rearrange.

  • Older teens (16-18 years old):

    Older teens are more independent and have a better sense of their own style. They may want to have more say in the design of their bedroom, and they may be more likely to choose furniture that reflects their personality and interests. Older teens may also need more storage space for clothes, books, and other belongings.

  • Teens with special needs:

    If your teen has special needs, you will need to choose furniture that is safe and accessible. For example, if your teen uses a wheelchair, you will need to choose furniture that is at a height that is easy for them to reach. You may also need to choose furniture with built-in safety features, such as rounded corners and non-slip surfaces.

  • Teens with allergies:

    If your teen has allergies, you will need to choose furniture that is made from hypoallergenic materials. For example, you may want to choose furniture made from wood or metal, rather than upholstered furniture. You should also avoid furniture that has a lot of dust-collecting surfaces.

By considering your teen's age, maturity level, and special needs, you can choose furniture that is both safe and stylish, and that will meet their needs for years to come.

Choose durable and sturdy pieces

When choosing teen bedroom furniture, it is important to choose pieces that are durable and sturdy. This is especially important for younger teens, who may be rougher on their furniture than older teens. Durable furniture will also last longer, which can save you money in the long run.

There are a few things to look for when choosing durable teen bedroom furniture. First, consider the materials used to make the furniture. Wood and metal are generally more durable than particle board or MDF. Second, look for furniture with solid construction. Avoid furniture that is made with flimsy materials or that has a lot of small pieces that could easily break.

You should also consider the finish of the furniture. A durable finish will protect the furniture from scratches and stains. Look for furniture with a finish that is easy to clean and maintain. You may also want to consider adding a protective coating to the furniture, such as a sealant or a wax.

Finally, be sure to choose furniture that is the right size for your teen's bedroom. Furniture that is too big or too small can be a safety hazard. It is also important to choose furniture that is proportional to the size of the room. Oversized furniture can make a small bedroom feel even smaller.

By choosing durable and sturdy teen bedroom furniture, you can create a space that is both stylish and safe, and that will last for years to come.

Consider space-saving furniture

If your teen's bedroom is small, you may need to consider space-saving furniture. Space-saving furniture is designed to maximize storage and functionality without taking up a lot of floor space.

  • Choose a loft bed:

    A loft bed is a great way to save space in a small bedroom. Loft beds have a raised sleeping area, which leaves space underneath for a desk, dresser, or other furniture.

  • Choose a Murphy bed:

    A Murphy bed is a bed that folds up into the wall when it is not in use. This is a great way to save space in a bedroom that is used for multiple purposes, such as a guest room or a home office.

  • Choose a trundle bed:

    A trundle bed is a bed that has a second bed that rolls out from underneath. This is a great way to accommodate sleepovers or extra guests.

  • Choose furniture with built-in storage:

    Furniture with built-in storage, such as beds with drawers or desks with shelves, can help you save space in a small bedroom. This type of furniture allows you to store items without taking up extra floor space.

By considering space-saving furniture, you can create a stylish and functional bedroom for your teen, even if the space is small.

Choose adjustable or convertible pieces

As your teen grows and changes, their needs will change as well. That's why it's a good idea to choose adjustable or convertible pieces of furniture that can grow with your teen.

  • Adjustable bed:

    An adjustable bed allows your teen to adjust the height of the bed frame and the angle of the headboard and footboard. This can be helpful for teens who have back or neck pain, or who simply want to be able to sit up in bed and read or watch TV.

  • Bunk beds:

By choosing adjustable or convertible pieces of furniture, you can create a bedroom that will meet your teen's needs for years to come.
