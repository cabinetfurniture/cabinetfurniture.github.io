Svara-Kembali

Svara-Kembali, literally meaning "self-returning," is a unique feature of Indonesian language that allows words or phrases to be repeated with a slight change in pronunciation or stress. This repetition can serve various grammatical and stylistic functions.

  • Penekanan (Emphasis)

    Repeating a word or phrase with increased stress emphasizes its importance or significance. For example:
    Saya sangat senang, sangat senang sekali bertemu dengan Anda.
    (I am very, very happy to meet you.)

  • Pengulangan (Repetition)

    Repeating a word or phrase without significant change in pronunciation or stress creates a sense of repetition or continuity. For example:
    Dia berlari, berlari, berlari tanpa henti.
    (He ran, and ran, and ran without stopping.)

  • Intensifikasi (Intensification)

    Repeating a word or phrase with a change in intonation or stress can intensify its meaning. For example:
    Saya tidak hanya suka, saya cinta kamu.
    (I don't just like you, I love you.)

  • Deskripsi (Description)

    Repeating a word or phrase with a change in pronunciation or stress can create a descriptive effect. For example:
    Daunnya hijau, hijau seperti zamrud.
    (The leaves are green, green like emeralds.)

Svara-Kembali adds richness and expressiveness to the Indonesian language, allowing speakers to convey subtle nuances of meaning and emotion.
